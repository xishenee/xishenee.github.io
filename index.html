About Me

I am currently a PhD candidate at the Lab for Atomic and Photonic Technologies at Northwestern University, supervised by Professor Selim M. Shahriar. My research focused on multidimensional holography, exploring applications such as ultra-fast image correlation and automatic event recognition. Additionally, I contribute to projects on photonic integrated circuits.

With extensive expertise in optical experiments, polymer fabrication, physics-based simulation, and mathematical modeling, I have a strong track record of interdisciplinary research in opto-electronic correlators, atomic physics, and photonic integrated circuits. My work has been published in leading venues, including CLEO, SPIE, FiO, AMOS, Optics Express, and Nature Nanotechnology.

As an aspiring optical research scientist, I aim to leverage my expertise to develop groundbreaking technologies that push the boundaries of light-based applications — from fundamental science to real-world innovations.
